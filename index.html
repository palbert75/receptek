Recipe № 01

Chicken
Paprikash

The Sunday classic: onions melted in fat, a fast swirl of red paprika, chicken legs braised slow, then finished with a silky flour-and-sour-cream temper.
≈ 1h 40mTotal time
BraiseMethod
Sour creamFinish
What you'll need
Onion Chicken legs Garlic Salt Black pepper Flour Sour cream Water Hungarian red paprika Pork fat (lard)
Step 01
01

Chop the onion

Finely chop a good amount of onion — it's the backbone of the whole sauce. Don't be shy with it.

Step 02
02

Joint the chicken legs

Separate the lower drumstick from the upper thigh at the joint. Cutting them apart helps the pieces cook evenly and fit the pan.

Step 03
03

Sweat onion in pork fat

Start heating the chopped onion with a spoon of pork fat in a big pot over medium heat. Let it soften slowly until glassy and translucent.

OptionalAdd 1–2 cloves of finely chopped garlic to the onion as it softens, for extra depth.
Step 04
04

Add the paprika — off the boil

Once the onion turns glassy, drop the heat to low and add a tablespoon of Hungarian red paprika.

Why low heatPaprika scorches in seconds, and burnt paprika turns the whole dish bitter.
Step 05
05

Stir it in, fast

Mix the paprika through the onions quickly so it coats everything and blooms into the fat without catching.

Step 06
06

Add the chicken legs

Nestle the jointed chicken pieces into the paprika-onion base, turning them to coat in the deep red color.

Step 07
07

Season with pepper

Grind black pepper over the chicken to taste.

Step 08
08

Then the salt

Add about a tablespoon of salt — adjust later once the sauce reduces.

Step 09
09

Pour in the water

Add 1½ cups of water. It will loosen the paprika base into the braising liquid that becomes your sauce.

Step 10
10

Cover & braise low

Put the lid on and let it cook gently for about 1 hour 20 minutes.

DonenessTest with a fork — the meat should be fork-tender and nearly falling off the bone.
Step 11
11

Start the thickener

In a separate bowl, measure out 2 tablespoons of flour.

Step 12
12

Add the sour cream

Stir 4 tablespoons of sour cream into the flour.

12·a
13

Loosen & mix smooth

Splash in a little water, then whisk the flour and sour cream until completely smooth with no lumps.

Temper
14

Temper it with the hot sauce

Ladle about 1½ cups of the hot chicken sauce from the pan into the flour–sour cream mix. Warming it gently this way stops the cream from splitting.

Step 15
15

Whisk until silky

Mix it well until the tempered cream is smooth and warm to the touch.

Finish
16

Return, boil, done

Pour the cream back into the pot and stir it through the chicken sauce. Let it boil for 4–5 minutes to thicken — and that's it, dinner's ready.

Serve it with

Nokedli, fresh bread, or a spoon of rice to catch every drop of that red sauce.

The side · made for the Paprikash

Nokedli

Soft little Hungarian egg dumplings — the traditional partner for paprikash, scooped straight into that red sour-cream sauce. No special skill needed, just a nokedli maker (or a colander with big holes). No photos for this one, so here's the recipe in full.

Serves 4
Ingredients
  • All-purpose flour 2 cups · ~250 g
  • Large eggs 2
  • Lukewarm water ½–¾ cup
  • Salt 1 tsp + more for the water
  • Sour cream 1–2 tbsp · optional
  • Oil or butter 1 tbsp · to finish
Method
  1. Boil the water. Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a rolling boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Make the batter. Whisk the eggs, salt and water (and sour cream, if using) in a large bowl. Gradually mix in the flour until you have a soft, sticky, slightly wet dough — like thick, lumpy pancake batter.
  3. Form the dumplings. Set a nokedli maker (or Spätzle press, or a large-holed grater) over the boiling water. Pour in a portion of batter and slide it back and forth to push the dough through into the water.
  4. Cook. The nokedli sink, then float to the surface. Let them boil about 1 minute after they float up.
  5. Drain & rinse. Lift them out with a slotted spoon, rinse briefly with cold water, drain, and toss with a little oil or melted butter so they don't stick.
  6. Repeat. Work in batches until all the batter is used up, then pile them next to the paprikash.
Recipe № 02

Lecsó

Summer in a pot: sweet peppers, onion and tomato stewed down with paprika until jammy and soft. Humble, vegetarian, and endlessly adaptable.
≈ 50mTotal time
StewMethod
Meat-freeAs-is
What you'll need
Onion Paprika peppers Tomato Salt Black pepper Hungarian red paprika Fat (lard or oil)
Step 01
01

Chop the onion

Same start as always — chop the onion. It melts down into the sweet base of the stew.

Step 02
02

Chop the peppers

Slice the peppers into strips or chunks.

Pepper noteTry to avoid bell pepper — it tastes noticeably different. Sweet Hungarian (wax) peppers are the real thing here.
Step 03
03

Chop the tomato

Roughly chop the tomato and set it aside — it goes in near the end.

Step 04
04

Sweat the onion

Heat the chopped onion with fat until it turns soft and glassy.

Step 05
05

Bloom the paprika

Add a tablespoon of Hungarian red paprika to the onion.

Low heatKeep the flame low — paprika burns fast and turns bitter.
Step 06
06

Add the peppers

Tip in the chopped peppers and turn them through the paprika onion.

Step 07
07

Season with pepper

Add black pepper to taste (and a pinch of salt if you like).

Step 08
08

Cover & soften

Stir, put the lid on, and cook for 20–30 minutes until the peppers go soft and start to collapse.

Step 09
09

Add the tomato

Stir the chopped tomato into the softened peppers.

Step 10
10

Mix it through

Stir well so the tomato breaks down into the sauce.

Finish
11

Cook until ready

Keep cooking until everything is soft, glossy and stewed together. Taste, adjust the salt, and serve.

Make it a meal

Crack in eggs at the end, or add sausage — but it's perfect with just bread, too.

Recipe № 03

Potato
Dumplings

Pillowy boiled dumplings made from riced potato and flour, rolled by hand and dropped into salted water until they bob to the top. A hearty, comforting side or a meal of their own.
≈ 45mTotal time
BoilMethod
6–7Dumplings
What you'll need
Floury potatoes Flour Salt
Step 01
01

Gather everything

Cook and drain your floury potatoes, then set out the flour and salt — along with a potato ricer and a slotted colander for later. That's all it takes: just potato, flour and salt.

Step 02
02

Rice the potatoes

Press the warm boiled potatoes through a ricer onto the board and let them cool a little. Pile the flour right alongside, ready to work in.

Step 03
03

Season with salt

Season the riced potato with salt — no egg, no butter, nothing else. Potato, flour and salt are all this dumpling needs.

Step 04
04

Knead the dough

Gradually fold the flour into the potato and knead just until it comes together into a soft, smooth dough — don't overwork it or it turns tough.

Step 05
05

Roll into balls

With lightly floured hands, divide the dough and roll it into even, smooth balls.

Optional · fried variationIf you like, roll the dough out about 1 cm thick instead, cut it into small round discs (little biscuit- or scone-shaped rounds, pogácsa style), and fry them in a little oil until golden brown and cooked through — crisp outside, soft inside.
Finish
06

Boil until they float

Lower the dumplings into a big pot of salted boiling water. They'll sink, then rise — once they float to the top, give them a few more minutes, then lift them out.

TipLift them out as soon as they're done and serve straight away alongside the paprikash.
Best served

Warm, with plenty of that paprika sauce spooned over the top.

Cook slow.
Stir with love.
